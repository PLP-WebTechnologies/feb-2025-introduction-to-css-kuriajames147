James Harrison

Under the gentle rustle of midnight's kiss, a city pulsates, a starry configuration of distant desires, Glass towers pierce the night's emptiness, whispering silences in shining gold and silver exhalations.
Reflected visions swirl upon the still river, waves of neon poetry pouring into eternity. The skyline, a diadem of light on the sleeping world a flicker of life that will never flag, never falter.
Time drifts in shadow and light here, where misplaced wanderers become disoriented in secrets uttered, where the rhythm of footsteps clears the path to silence, and the city, ever watchful, never sleeps.
